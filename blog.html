Lab 2: Contextual Inquiry

After watching the new movie Deep Water Horizon, our group was inspired to examine a hypothetical oil spill in the Puget Sounds area. We decided that the potential cause of this event was the spill of a massive oil container into the water. This event woud be extremely harsh on the marine and wildlife in the area, along with the fishing economy. All shipping and transportation in this area would be halted, and the Seattle port would suffer during this event.


We decided to interview:

  • Oil spill cleanup manager
  • Marine Biologist
  • Shipping Manager

Informal Questions

  • What kind of long-lasting effects should we expect in the affected area?
  • What kind of immediate effects should we expect?
  • What sort of technology is available to handle these types of disasters?
  • What is the impact of this kind of disaster on your line of work?
  • What sort of legislation has resulted from this disaster in the past?

Formal Questions:

  • What is the time frame for cleaning up an oil spill?
  • What kind of cost would you expect to see from this disaster?
  • What are the best methods for recovery?
  • Who will be most impacted?
  • What is the first step in responding to this disaster?

Survey Questions

  • Describe the shortcomings you experienced last time when handling this type of disaster.
  • What is the most limiting factor in handling this kind of disaster?/li>
  • Rate your level of direct involvement with the clean up of the spill.
  • What are your recommendations for approaching this disaster in the future?
  • What are your recommendations for preventing this disaster in the future?

Lab 3: User Research

In todays lab we were asked to choose a topic that we know is a common issue, adn then explore that topic. We chose the issue of college kids not knowing how to cook. We chose this issue due to the fact that there could be several reasons as to why a student does know how to cook for themselves or as to why they refuse to learn. OVerall from our surveys, we learned that budget, time, and skill play a huge roll into this issue as college kids tend to not have money, are busy with school, and were never taught to cook prior to college.


Summary Of our user feedback

Two of my group members were in charge of presenting our issue to other people and recording their results. We had identified that users affected by this problem either didn't have access to a kitchen, or if they did, they just didnt really use it to its full potential. Often times our surveyers ate out, or just purchased frozen food to save time, or because they just didnt have the skills to make a nice meal. However this a costly option for them, as purchasing pre made meals can get expensive.


Design Ideas

  • Create an app that would show students simple one pot recipes, cooking techniques, ad cooking classes
  • Cookbook that included one pot, four ingredient recipes, with photos and detailed instruction of every meal.

Failure Reflection

What messages do we receive that its not okay to fail?

There are several ways of people telling others that it's not okay to fail. Alot of these messages tend to be direct, as failure can be of great importance to some people and therefore make an effort to let you know that it is not okay to fail. One example of this is my parents who constantly have applied pressure on me througout my life to ensure I succeed in school. I would get rewarded with good grades, but scolded with bad grades. Similarly I can imagine this being popular in alot of other households where school is important to the childs parents. There is also internal messages such as seeing other people around you succeed and you feeling the pressure to do the same.

What is the value of failure (personally, in society, in design)? What are the risks?

Failure is extremely important. When someone fails, they are forced to reflect on what they did wrong, and they learn to problem solve and go about the issue in a way they didnt before. This is true personally, in society, and in design.
The risks of failing is that you can be affected personally by a failure, or get discourages from getting back up and trying again.

When is failure okay for you personally? At what point is failure not okay?

Personally, I try to avoid failure at all costs, especially with things that do matter to me. This is true in school, and my business ventures of owning a few companies and doing freelance work for different clients. Failure is okay to me when I fail at something irrelevant or have another shot without any risks. Failure is not okay when I am dealing with clients or working on school projects, or on something I know needs to be perfect the first time.

Whats something you procrastinate doing or don’t do at all because you’re afraid you might fail?

Right now it is currently taking my online technical tests for internships. I have one shot and they are for big companies I would love to work for. Unfortunately I am afraid of failing them so Ive put them off so I can re learn my java skills and programming skills.

If your boss or teacher told you to fail on purpose how would you do it?
